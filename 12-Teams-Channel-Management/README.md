## 12 - Microsoft Teams Channel Management

Channels in Microsoft Teams are dedicated sections within a team workspace used to organize conversations, files, and collaboration by topic or purpose. Every team comes with a default General channel, but additional channels can be created to keep specific discussions and work organized. In this lab a Standard channel was created inside the IT Help Desk Teams workspace to track escalated tickets and simulate how IT teams organize their collaboration spaces.

## Step 4: Configure the Channel Details

Filled in the channel details:

- **Channel name:** Ticket Escalations
- **Description:** Channel for tracking and discussing escalated IT support tickets that require Tier 2 or higher attention
- **Channel type:** Standard — accessible to all members of the IT Help Desk team

A Standard channel is visible to all team members. A Private channel would restrict access to only specific members within the team. Since all IT Help Desk staff need visibility into escalated tickets, Standard was the appropriate choice here.

Channel management is a routine Microsoft Teams administration task. Creating well-organized channels helps IT teams separate different types of work — general communication, ticket tracking, escalations, and documentation — so nothing gets lost in a single crowded channel. This is a skill helpdesk and sysadmin teams use regularly when onboarding new teams or reorganizing existing workspaces.
